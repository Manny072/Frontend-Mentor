Nigerian Egusi Soup Recipe

Egusi Soup is a rich and flavorful Nigerian dish made with ground melon seeds, leafy vegetables, and assorted meats. Itâ€™s often enjoyed with pounded yam, eba, or fufu.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 60 minutes
  • Preparation: 20 minutes
  • Cooking: 40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 cups egusi (ground melon seeds)
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cups beef or goat meat, cooked
  • 1 cup assorted meats (shaki, kpomo, etc.)
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 2 cups stock or water
  • 2 cups chopped spinach or bitter leaf
  • 2 tablespoons locust beans (iru) (optional)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers, blended
  • 1 teaspoon ground pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 seasoning cube

Instructions

  1. Prepare the egusi paste: Mix the ground egusi with some water to form a thick paste.
  2. Heat the palm oil: In a pot, heat palm oil on medium heat and fry the chopped onions for a minute.
  3. Cook the egusi: Add the egusi paste and fry for about 10 minutes while stirring.
  4. Add stock and meat: Pour in the stock, followed by the cooked meats, crayfish, locust beans, and blended peppers.
  5. Simmer: Let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. Add vegetables: Stir in the spinach or bitter leaf and cook for another 5 minutes.
  7. Serve: Enjoy with pounded yam, eba, or fufu.
